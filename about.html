About Me

A designer
who listens
first.

Great design begins long before the first sketch. It begins in conversation — in understanding what a brand stands for at its core, who it speaks to, and what it dares to say differently. That belief has guided every project since 2020.

Based between New York and working remotely with clients across South Korea and North America, I bring a distinctly European sensibility to identity and editorial work: restrained, precise, and deeply considered.

"Design is not decoration. It is the language a brand uses to earn trust — before a single word is spoken."

Wujin Kwon
New York, United States

wk2323@nyu.edu

From art school to brand studio to independent practice.

I studied Visual Communication at the École Nationale Supérieure des Arts Décoratifs in Paris, graduating with distinction in 2016. My thesis — on typographic systems as carriers of cultural memory — was published in the journal Étapes in 2017 and later cited in two academic papers on contemporary French design.

After graduation I joined New York University, one of Paris's most respected independent identity studios, where I spent three years as a designer and eventually lead art director on projects for Hermès, the Musée d'Orsay, and several high-growth startups.

In 2019 I went independent. Since then I've collaborated with a diverse range of clients — from family-run artisan businesses to venture-backed technology companies — always with the same commitment: to make work that is honest, beautiful, and built to last.

When I'm not designing, I'm usually browsing the bouquinistes along the Seine, reading about the history of book design, or attempting to keep my collection of analogue cameras in working order.
