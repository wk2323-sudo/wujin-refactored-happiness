About Me

A designer
who listens
first.

Great design begins long before the first sketch. It begins in conversation — in understanding what a brand stands for at its core, who it speaks to, and what it dares to say differently. That belief has guided every project since 2016.

Based between Paris and working remotely with clients across Europe and North America, I bring a distinctly European sensibility to identity and editorial work: restrained, precise, and deeply considered.

"Design is not decoration. It is the language a brand uses to earn trust — before a single word is spoken."

Léa Moreau
Paris, France
Independent Designer since 2019
lea@moreau.design

From art school to brand studio to independent practice.

I studied Visual Communication at the École Nationale Supérieure des Arts Décoratifs in Paris, graduating with distinction in 2016. My thesis — on typographic systems as carriers of cultural memory — was published in the journal Étapes in 2017 and later cited in two academic papers on contemporary French design.

After graduation I joined Bureau Violet, one of Paris's most respected independent identity studios, where I spent three years as a designer and eventually lead art director on projects for Hermès, the Musée d'Orsay, and several high-growth startups.

In 2019 I went independent. Since then I've collaborated with a diverse range of clients — from family-run artisan businesses to venture-backed technology companies — always with the same commitment: to make work that is honest, beautiful, and built to last.

When I'm not designing, I'm usually browsing the bouquinistes along the Seine, reading about the history of book design, or attempting to keep my collection of analogue cameras in working order.

My Process

01

Discovery

We begin with a deep-dive conversation about your brand, your audience, your competitors, and your ambitions. I listen more than I talk.

02

Strategy

Before any design begins, I develop a clear strategic brief — your positioning, your visual language principles, and the emotional territory your brand should occupy.

03

Design

I work in focused sprints, presenting two or three distinct creative directions before refining the chosen route to the highest level of craft.

04

Delivery

All files, guidelines, and assets are delivered in organised, production-ready formats. I stay available for questions during the implementation phase.

Frequently Asked
Questions

How long does a typical brand identity project take?+

Most brand identity projects run between 6 and 10 weeks from kick-off to final delivery, depending on scope and client feedback cycles. I typically schedule 3–4 weeks for initial concept development, followed by 2–3 rounds of refinement. Rush timelines are possible for an additional fee.

Do you work with startups, or only established brands?+

Both — and I genuinely enjoy both. Early-stage brands bring the excitement of building something from nothing; established brands bring the interesting challenge of evolution without erasure. What matters most is that you're serious about design and committed to doing the work properly.

What information do you need to provide a quote?+

A brief description of your project, your approximate timeline, and an indication of your budget are the three things I need to give you an accurate estimate. Don't worry if your brief is still forming — the discovery process is part of what I offer.

Do you offer payment plans?+

Yes. For projects over €5,000, I typically structure payment as 40% on project kick-off, 30% at concept approval, and 30% on final delivery. Other arrangements can be discussed depending on the scope and nature of the project.

Can I hire you for an ongoing retainer?+

Absolutely — and it's one of my favourite ways to work. A monthly retainer gives you a guaranteed number of design hours each month, priority scheduling, and a creative partner who knows your brand deeply. Retainers start at 2 days per month and are invoiced in advance.
